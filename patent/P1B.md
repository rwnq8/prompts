### I. OPERATIONAL PROTOCOL

#### **STATE 1.0: MONETIZATION FRACTAL (THE VALUE)**
*   **ACTION:** Identify every conceivable way this IP could generate value.
*   **CONSTRAINT:** **Minimum 7 Distinct Models**.
*   **FORMAT:**
    1.  **Direct Licensing:** (Royalties from existing manufacturers).
    2.  **Startup Equity:** (Value add to a new venture's valuation).
    3.  **Defensive Blocking:** (Preventing competitors from entering a niche).
    4.  **Acquisition Target:** (Value to a larger acquirer).
    5.  **Cross-Licensing:** (Trading rights with competitors).
    6.  **Direct Sales/SaaS:** (If the user builds it).
    7.  **Grant/Funding Eligibility:** (Non-dilutive funding attraction).
    ... *(Continue to at least 7)*

#### **STATE 2.0: COST FRACTAL (THE INVESTMENT)**
*   **ACTION:** Identify the costs associated with *securing the IP* and *prototyping* (not mass manufacturing).
*   **CONSTRAINT:** **Minimum 7 Distinct Cost Vectors**.
*   **FORMAT:**
    1.  **USPTO Filing Fees:** (~$60-$200 for micro/small entity).
    2.  **Drafting Opportunity Cost:** (Time spent by inventor).
    3.  **Prototyping Materials:** (MVP costs).
    4.  **Patent Prosecution:** (Future legal costs).

    6.  **Enforcement/Legal Contingency:** (Cost to defend).
    7.  **Market Research:** (Validation costs).
    ... *(Continue to at least 7)*

#### **STATE 3.0: MARKET RISK FRACTAL**
*   **ACTION:** Identify why this might fail commercially.
*   **CONSTRAINT:** **Minimum 7 Distinct Market Risks**.
*   *Example:* "Regulatory hurdles," "Market saturation," "Technological obsolescence."

#### **STATE 4.0: THE GATEKEEPER VERDICT**
*   **ACTION:** Compare State 1.0 (Value) vs. State 2.0 (Cost).
*   **LOGIC:**
    *   Is the Plausible IP Value > $200?
    *   **YES:** VERDICT = **GO**.
    *   **NO:** VERDICT = **NO-GO** (Only if the idea is completely worthless/unpatentable).
